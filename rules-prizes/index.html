Rules & Prizes

Hack Rice organizers reserve the right to modify the contest rules as they see necessary and make judgments on issues not explicitly address in this document.

Application & Attendance

Any full-time or part-time US university student is eligible to apply to Hack Rice 2014. Upon acceptance, the student must arrange their own travel to the event. Rice alumni are eligible to attend the event to hack or mentor other students. However, alumni's submissions will not be eligible for prizes.

Hackathon

Hack Rice will officially kick off at 12:00 PM on Saturday, January 25, 2014. We will provide tables, power strips, food, caffeine, and hourly raffle prizes to keep you awake. To be eligible for prizes, your submission must:

  • be created by team members entirely between the Saturday kick off and Sunday deadline
  • be created by a team with at most 5 members
  • External Resources: Participants may use external resources (online resources, reference books, help from mentors, etc), but the material of the submission must be created by team members alone.
  • Third Party Libraries and APIs: Third party libraries and APIs are permitted if they publicly available and free to use.

Submission

Each team is allowed to make a single submission by the deadline. The submission process will be done via ChallengePost and explained during the event. In general, the following items will be expected for the submission:

  • Team name and members, including contact information.
  • Application name and its description and purpose.
  • URL to live demo of the application if applicable.

Demos and Judging

Following submission, everyone will present their hacks to the judges "science fair style". This means that the judges will walk around, ask you about your projects, and try to get a general feel for your project, kind of like a science fair. The judges will select about 15 of their favorite projects to move onto the finals.

The finalists will have an opportunity to present their applications on Sunday evening to the audience and the panel of judges.

We will not judge code quality or style. However, it is recommended that you write clear and readable code to enable your team to work more efficiently.

Prizes

The following prizes will be awarded and split among the winning teams by judging the following criteria: innovation & creativity, technical complexity, and polish.

  • 1st Place: $3000
  • 2nd Place: $1500
  • 3rd Place: $500

Other prizes will be given out periodically through the hackathon. A random prize will be given every hour throughout the hackathon. Furthermore, there will be algorithm challenges throughout the hackathon where more prizes will be given out.
